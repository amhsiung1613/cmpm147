Experiment 02 - Living Impressions

Description

This project offers a generative, living impression of the above image. In this experiment, I attempted to transform relevant portions of my reference image onto my blank digital canvas. In order to create a dynamic and textured landscape, I used p5.js, a JavaScript library for creative coding. I took inspiration from a multitude of sources, including my professor, Wes Modes, utilizing the mountain formation code from his example project as a starting point. To better represent the parabolic curve of the coast, I experimented with bezier curves and coordinates. In order to imitate the following landforms, I scaled the existing parabolic curve coordinates to be a better match.

Technical

In order to introduce an ebb and flow motion to the water, I coded the coordinates so that they move along a sin wave every frame.This was a little harder than I originally expected since the parabola would occasionally come too far on-screen or go too far off-screen. I needed to play with the numbers for a while before getting the perfect calculations. This allowed the water to look like a tide, coming inland and flowing out to sea every so often.

I also implemented a tracking mechanism where the wave will slightly follow the horizontal movement of the mouse, adding some variety into the ebb and flow of the wave. This was done by having the coordinates of the parabola be affected by the x-position of the mouse. The calculations for this were interesting in that it could greatly affect the ebb and flow of the wave if the factor wasn't scaled correctly.

Reflection

My original idea was too difficult to implement. I had wanted to incorporate a Perlin noise wave in a curve, to further imitate the ebb and flow of tides. Unfortunately, this was much more difficult than I had originally anticipated. I ultimately decided to imitate the general shape of the shore instead, with ebb and flow motions as my replacement Perlin noise wave. That, fortunately, works pretty well. I had also wanted to use a static Perlin noise wave to define the rocks and the grass but I couldn't figure out how to rotate a Perlin noise wave.

If I had more time, I would add more texture to the rocks, the grass, and the edge of the waves, where it hits the shore.
